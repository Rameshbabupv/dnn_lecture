üß† Understanding Linear Separability

  • AND Gate: The blue line successfully separates all red points (output=0) from the green point (output=1). A single-layer perceptron can learn this!
  • OR Gate: The blue line separates the red point (output=0) from all green points (output=1). Also learnable by a single-layer perceptron!
  • XOR Gate: No straight line can separate the red points from green points - they form a checkerboard pattern. This requires a multi-layer perceptron with hidden layers!

üöÄ Neural Network Implications

  • Linearly Separable Problems (AND, OR): Can be solved with a single neuron (perceptron)
  • Non-Linearly Separable Problems (XOR): Require hidden layers to create complex decision boundaries
  • Historical Significance: The XOR problem was a major criticism of early neural networks until multi-layer perceptrons were developed
